## Technology & Pathways

### 1. Enhanced Rock Weathering (ERW) — Darjeeling Revival Project (DRP)
The DRP is a first-of-its-kind effort to unite climate action with cultural and ecological restoration. We source waste basalt rock from mines and spread it across agricultural fields. This volcanic rock improves soil health and crop yields while reacting naturally with rainwater to remove CO₂. When CO₂ in rainwater interacts with basalt dust, it converts into stable bicarbonate ions stored in soil, eventually traveling through river networks to the ocean as calcium carbonate (CaCO₃) for over 10,000 years.

**Key facts:**
- Darjeeling's climate (high summer temps ~25-35°C, humidity 75-90%, monsoonal rainfall 2500-3000 mm/year) produces some of the highest natural silicate weathering rates globally
- Direct proximity to Rajmahal Traps provides billions of tons of high-reactivity basalt at a fraction of US/European costs
- Projected cost of $68/ton by 2030 — significantly ahead of Lithos Carbon ($220) and Undo ($200)
- First ERW project in Asia validated under Isometric standard
- 60,000+ acres onboarded across four agro-climatic zones and two major crop types
- Capacity to issue 100,000 carbon credits from 2026

### 2. Biochar — Bengal Renaissance Project (BRP)
The BRP converts agricultural residues into biochar through pyrolysis and deploys it directly into farmland soil across South Asia. Biochar locks carbon away for 1,000+ years while improving soil health.

**Key facts:**
- Isometric-audited, targeting the 1,000-year permanence tier
- GIS-mapped biomass supply chains across multiple districts
- Collection depots and pyrolysis plant converting residues at regional scale
- Digital twin of the biochar lifecycle: feedstock procurement → pyrolysis → application → carbon permanence

### 3. Planetary Intelligence Platform
Mission to build underlying agri-infrastructure that turns fragmented observations about soils, water & climate systems into unified, actionable understanding of Earth.

**Key infrastructure:**
- **FELUDA:** End-to-end digital workflow — farm boundary mapping, farmer onboarding, heterogeneity assessment, sampling point navigation, QR-coded sample tracking, chain-of-custody, lab QC, real-time CDR calculations
- **The Observatory:** 2M+ structured data points covering soil chemistry & biology, texture, bulk density, porewater geochemistry, weather patterns, river discharge, biomass/plant uptake. Target: 170M+ datapoints by 2030
- **South Asia Soil Atlas:** Mapping soil chemistry, carbon saturation, microbial activity, crop response across 100+ agro-climatic zones

## MRV (Measurement, Reporting & Verification) — The Moat

Alt Carbon's most defensible strategic advantage is our MRV system. We see MRV not just as a requirement, but as the foundation for IP, market access, premium pricing, and defensibility at scale.

**MRV capabilities:**
- In-house system integrates real-time reactor analytics, batch-specific lab assays, traceability, and GIS-tagged field application data
- Goes significantly beyond Isometric protocol minimums
- Every batch tagged with: production conditions (feedstock type, temperature, residence time, gas flow), carbon properties (CEC, BET surface area, moisture, ash, pH, heavy metals), application context (soil type, use case, crop history, geographic zone)
- Enables "MRV-as-a-Service" for third-party deployments

**Darjeeling Climate Action Lab (D-CAL):**
- State-of-the-art Earth Sciences laboratory inside a heritage tea estate
- Expanded to 15,000 sqft (from 1,800 sqft in 2024)
- Equipment: ICP-MS, ICP-OES, XRD, CHNS, IRMS, XRF
- Processing capacity: up to 100,000 samples annually by 2026
- High-throughput pipelines processing thousands of soil, rock, water samples weekly at global QA/QC standards

**MRV Methodology (Isometric standard):**
- Solid-phase measurements of base cation loss (Ca²⁺, Mg²⁺) over time with Titanium (Ti) as insoluble tracer
- Net CO₂ calculated after accounting for biomass uptake and loss deductions
- Stratified random sampling (1 soil sample/ha), 15 sub-points per hectare
- Validated using Kullback-Leibler divergence and Pareto optimization
- Annual follow-up sampling with ≥50m spacing for spatial independence
- Porewater monitoring via macrorhizons at 30cm depth

- Low leakage potential (10-20%) based on basin-specific ocean carbonate chemistry
